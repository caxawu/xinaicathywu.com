Spaces

Designer

Spaces is a VR perspective puzzle game based on M.C. Escher's "Relativity"
drawing. Players jump to different walls to match their perspective of the room
to the perspective shown in a series of pictures.

My contribution
I mainly focused on design for this project which included:
Asset creation
I 3D modelled the Escher room itself based off of M.C. Escher's drawing. I also modelled the gamified elements like the flower vines and the lantern that appear when finishing a puzzle.

Animation
To make the scene transition between the tutorial room and the Escher room cooler, instead of fading to black and fading black in to a new environment, I animated the environment change around the player to make the whole experience feel more cohesive.
To incentive players to keep playing and to create delight, I animated the flower vines to grow.

Lighting
Another key component to the user experience was the lighting. I lit the main Escher room and added god rays to make the scene more interesting. I added a glow effect to the flowers and the lantern as well to increase visual appeal.
The process
Initially, my partner and I brainstormed a whole range of different project ideas for our culminating project. We considered everything from a 2D animation to an interactive immersive story in VR. But, we knew that if we were to build something in VR, we wanted it to take full advantage of VR's capabilities and make something unique.

While researching inspiration for one of our ideas, we came across M.C. Escher's "Relativity" drawing. We were inspired to incorporate a perspective shift into our idea, but found that this play on perspective was easily the most compelling component. So, we decided to pivot and bring Escher's drawing to life instead.


By bringing it to life in VR, players would be able to truly experience the perspective changes that "Relativity" captures.


However, rather than simply allowing players to observe Escher's world, we wanted to engage them. To encourage them to explore the room, we came up with perspective puzzles.
