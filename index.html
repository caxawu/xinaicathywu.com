About me!
I spent my childhood wading through the pages of arts and crafts books—eager to find new projects to try. From making origami necklaces to trying to drawing my favorite Pokemon (which turned out to be, at best, misshapen), my love of art and design grew with me. But even as I began taking art classes to hone my skills, I always thought of it as a hobby and never something I would pursue as a career.
I discovered experience design during my sophomore year at Dartmouth College. All of a sudden, I found my creative home in tech. This world of impactful design has intrigued and inspired me to major in Cognitive Science focused in Human-Computer Interaction and minor in Human-Centered Design and Digital Arts.
Now as a senior, I am creating experiences for projects ranging from web apps to VR games. I am excited to apply my knowledge and to grow as a designer as I prepare to make the leap from college to a full-time career.
Background
I was born in Singapore, lived in China and Canada briefly, but grew up in Rhode Island.
Last year, I lived in Lyon, France for 3 months during a language immersion program.
I speak English, Chinese, and (kind of) French!
