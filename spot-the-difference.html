Spot-the-Difference


Skills:
Tools:
Time:
Brainstorming, Prototyping
Maya, Unity
2 Weeks





Spot-the-Difference is a reimagination of the classic game in VR. Bringing it to a 3D space allows us to take advantage of the exploratory nature of the game as players can walk around two scenes and discover different elements.

apk for Oculus Go




 The project brief

Work in a team of two to create any VR experience that is something meaningful, informative, beautiful, or all three. Try to not have the three dimensional spatial aspect of VR be an irrelevant gimmick but rather something more intrinsic where the overall user experience is delightful.





 The inspiration

Because there were so many directions we could take this project, my partner and I first thought of ways in which VR could add to an experience.


We found our way to spot-the-difference games and thought to reimagining this static 2D game into an animated 3D one.






 The goal


Our goal for this project was to create a fun and delightful experience that combines nostalgia from classic spot-the-difference games with the newest innovations in technology with VR.





 The app


Players explore the world in the real scene, then head over to the difference scene to try to spot the differences.





When the game starts, the player is in a lobby where they have the option of visiting either the actual scene or the differences scene.

In the real scene, the player can walk around and explore the world:


The swaying trees have squirrels and birds idling in the branches.
The pond contains a fish swimming around while lilypads float on top.
Grass, flowers, and mushroom are scattered throughout on the ground while a hot air balloon and butterfly float overhead.

In the differences scene, the player has 5 lives to guess all 6 differences:

The player uses the trigger on the controller to point at an object in the scene.
If they are correct in identifying an extra object, the object turns green.
If they are wrong, they lose a life and the object turns red.

 The takeaways


This project was a lot of fun to work on. I was able to experiment with how to use a 3-dimensional space in a productive way which allowed me to really appreciate what an extra dimension could add to an experience. I also found that an experience can still be very immersive even with a rather simplistic aesthetic like low-poly.
