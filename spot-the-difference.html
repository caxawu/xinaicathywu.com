Spot-the-Difference

Designer

Spot-the-Difference is a reimagination of the classic game in VR. Bringing it to a 3D space allows us to take advantage of the exploratory nature of the game as players can walk around two scenes and discover different elements.

My contribution
I was the designer on this project which encompassed:
Asset creation
I 3D modelled almost all of the assets used in a low-poly style through Maya.

Scene design
I pulled my 3D models into Unity to create a cohesive nature-y environment that looked appealing and fun to be in. I also animated most of the assets and added sound to the environment to make it even more immersive.

Experience design
While making this game, I focused on the user’s experience of playing it to make sure that the game was fun and the experience was delightful.
The project brief
The assignment tasked us with creating any VR experience that is meaningful, informative, beautiful, or all three. We were guided by the mission of trying not have the three dimensional spatial aspect of VR be an irrelevant gimmick but rather something more intrinsic where the overall user experience is delightful.
The inspiration
Because there were so many directions we could take this project, my partner and I first thought of ways in which VR could add to an experience. We wanted to take full advantage of the immersiveness and the access to a third dimension in VR.

When considering games for inspiration, we found our way to spot-the-difference games. Most notably, these games are incredibly simple, but they have always been static and 2D. So, we thought to reimagining it into an animated 3D one where the player could be dropped into the world and actually walk around and explore the environment.


Our goal: Create a fun and delightful experience that combines nostalgia from classic spot-the-difference games with the newest innovations in technology with VR.
The walkthrough
Players start off in a mini lobby scene with options to go into two scenes. They can explore the real scene first, then head into the differences scene to try and spot the elements that are different.

The low poly island contains:
Swaying trees with squirrels and birds idling in the branches.
A pond with a fish swimming around while lilypads float on top.
Grass, flowers, and mushrooms are occasionally visited by a butterfly while a hot air balloon floats overhead.
