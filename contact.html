Contact

Skills: User Research, Interviewing, Needfinding, Storyboarding
Tools: Final Cut Pro

The prompt

Imagine the experience of work/learning/recreation/parenting/the arts in the year 2025. Explore how to improve this experience with interactive digital technology.

What we chose

My team chose to focus on learning and began by researching existing trends and technologies. After multiple mind maps and interviews with the Student Accessibility Office we targeted several pain points and began brainstorming way in which we could improve them.

The problem

We thought about how we might more easily and more seamlessly connect with people who speak a different language. Ultimately, we set our sights on how individuals who does not speak American Sign Language need to communicate with hearing impaired individuals in order to form emotional connections.


Our answer




We envisioned a product that takes contact lenses to the next level. With translating technologies built in, it provides real-time translations—allowing people who do not speak the same language to communicate seamlessly. In today's world where technology is becoming more and more prominent, we wanted to use technology to bring people together and foster connection.

Future uses and next steps


Foreign countries
Besides helping those who do not know sign language, this same technology can be used as a faster version of Google Translate, allowing people to feel more comfortable when travelling. This can help people feel less overwhelmed and safer in an unfamiliar location.

Classrooms
The technology would have applications in the classroom as well. Students with hearing disabilities and students sitting in the back of a big lecture hall could see what the professor is saying without distractions from other students.

Loud environments
People could better communicate with their friends at concerts and other noisy environments without having to shout. This contributes to people staying safe and aware.
