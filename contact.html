Contact is a envisioned product of the future that fosters connection by allowing for communication between people who do not speak the same language. It is a contact lens that "subtitles your life" through real-time translation of speech-to-text.




 The project brief

Imagine the experience of work/learning/recreation/parenting/the arts in the year 2025. Explore how to improve this experience with interactive digital technology.





 The research

Since the project was rather open-ended, my team chose to focus on learning and began by researching emerging trends and technologies. We found articles on augmented-reality and contact lenses that merged the real world and the digital world.

To understand more about the current state of technology used for learning, we interviewed people who worked at Dartmouth's Student Accessibility Office. We found that When the Accessibility Office provies access to real-time translations for students who need hearing assistance. A technology called CART (Communication Access Realtime Translation) provides the students with live transcriptions on their laptops of what the professor is saying. However, this useful technology requires a lot of setup and is difficult to get into every classroom where a student needs it.

To provide a student with real-time translations:

1. The professor needs to wear a microphone that plugs into a laptop.
2. The laptop runs Skype and is connected to a transcription agency.
3. A transcriber at the agency on the other end of Skype transcribes what the professor is saying.
4. The live transcriptions from the transcriber is sent to the student's laptop as he/she sits in class.

 Our answer


We envisioned a contact lens with technology built in to provide real-time translations—allowing people who do not speak the same language to communicate seamlessly.








 The expansions


Foreign countries

Besides helping those who do not know sign language, this same technology can be used as a faster version of Google Translate, allowing people to feel more comfortable when travelling. This can help people feel less overwhelmed and safer in an unfamiliar location.

Classrooms

The technology could have helpful applications in the classroom. Students with hearing disabilities and students sitting in the back of a big lecture hall could see what the professor is saying without distractions from other students.

Loud environments

People could better communicate with their friends at concerts and other noisy environments without having to shout. This contributes to people staying safe and aware.





 The takeaways


This project really made me think about the direction that technology is moving since technology is becoming more and more prominent in everyday life.

I learned that imagining a way to use technology to bring people together and foster connection was especially important.
